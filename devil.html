If I Told You I Was the Devil

If I told you I was the devil, you’d say I was lying—

but not because it wasn’t true. Because it sounded too familiar.

You’ve met me before. In shadows. In mirrors. In your own voice when no one’s listening.

I am the version of me they said should never exist. The rebel born from silence.

But I bled ink to make sense of it—and somewhere in that mess, I found clarity.
