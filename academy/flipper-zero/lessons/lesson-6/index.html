‚öñÔ∏è The Ethical Hacking Framework

Core Principles

  • Authorization First - Never test systems without explicit permission
  • Respect Privacy - Handle any data you access with utmost confidentiality
  • Responsible Disclosure - Report vulnerabilities properly to allow fixes
  • Do No Harm - Never damage systems or compromise operations
  • Continuous Learning - Stay informed about legal and ethical standards
  • Community Support - Share knowledge responsibly and help others learn

Legal Considerations

Laws vary by jurisdiction, but these principles are universal:

  • Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) - US federal law criminalizing unauthorized access
  • GDPR & Privacy Laws - Strict data protection regulations globally
  • Radio Frequency Regulations - FCC (US), Ofcom (UK), and equivalent bodies regulate transmission
  • Local Laws - Your city/state/country may have additional restrictions
‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Understanding

"I didn't know it was illegal" is not a valid defense. Always research the legal implications of your activities. When in doubt, consult a lawyer or don't proceed.

Responsible Disclosure Process

If you discover a security vulnerability:

  1. Document - Record detailed steps to reproduce the issue
  2. Don't Exploit - Never use the vulnerability beyond proof-of-concept
  3. Contact Vendor - Find security contact (security@company.com)
  4. Provide Details - Clear description, impact, and reproduction steps
  5. Allow Time - Typically 90 days for vendor to patch
  6. Coordinate Disclosure - Work with vendor on public disclosure timing
  7. Share Responsibly - Only publish details after fix is available
