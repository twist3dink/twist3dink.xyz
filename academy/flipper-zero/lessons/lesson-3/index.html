Lesson 3: Infrared (Record → Save → Replay)

Estimated time: 20–30 minutes | XP Reward: 50

Objective

Get your first clean, undeniable win: record IR from a remote you own, save it, and replay it successfully. If this lesson works, you’ll trust your workflow for the rest of the track.

Scope + safety:
  • Only record/replay IR for devices you own or have explicit permission to control.
  • IR is line-of-sight. If it doesn’t work, it’s usually angle/distance—not magic.

Prereqs

  • Flipper Zero
  • Any IR remote you own (TV remote, LED lights, fan, AC, projector, etc.)
  • Your target device in the same room
Reminder: Don’t point this at public TVs or other people’s devices “as a joke.” That’s how you become the villain in someone else’s story.

IR Basics (what matters)

Infrared remotes transmit bursts of light (invisible to your eyes) at a carrier frequency. Your device decodes patterns like “Volume Up” or “Power.”

What makes IR easy

  • Most consumer IR commands are simple to record and replay.
  • No radio regulations, no pairing, no rolling codes (in most basic remotes).

What makes IR fail

  • Bad alignment (not pointing at the IR receiver)
  • Too far away
  • Bright sunlight washing out the receiver
  • Some AC remotes use long/stateful messages (still doable, just more finicky)

Step-by-step: Record → Save → Replay

Step 1 — Find the IR app/menu

  1. From the main menu, open Infrared.
  2. Look for the option to Learn new remote / Learn / Record.

Step 2 — Record 3 buttons

Pick commands that give obvious feedback. Recommended set:

  • Power (only if it’s safe to toggle)
  • Volume Up
  • Volume Down
  1. Start “learn/record” on Flipper.
  2. Hold your remote 2–6 inches from the Flipper’s IR receiver area.
  3. Press and release the button once.
  4. Repeat for a total of 3 buttons.
Practical tip: If it fails, don’t spam buttons. Adjust distance/angle first, then try again.

Step 3 — Save as a named remote profile

  1. Save your learned remote with a name like:
    • tv-livingroom or led-strip-bedroom
  2. Confirm it appears under Infrared → Saved (or similar).

Step 4 — Replay and confirm it works

  1. Open your saved remote profile.
  2. Point the Flipper at the device’s IR receiver (usually near the screen edge or a small dark window).
  3. Send Volume Up, then Volume Down.
  4. Confirm the device responds.
Don’t “test” Power repeatedly if it interrupts someone’s setup or causes issues. Use volume/channel/light brightness for safer verification.

Deliverables (Proof Pack)

To finish Lesson 3, you must produce these artifacts. No proof, no completion.

  • D3.1 — A photo/screenshot showing your saved IR remote profile name on the Flipper.
  • D3.2 — Evidence the replay worked (choose one):
    • Short video of the device responding, or
    • Two photos: before/after (e.g., volume level indicator, light brightness change)
  • D3.3 — A 2–5 sentence note:
    • What device did you control?
    • What commands did you record?
    • What failed (if anything) and what fixed it?

Verification

  • I have a saved IR profile with a meaningful name.
  • I can replay at least two commands and see the device respond.
  • I have D3.1, D3.2, and D3.3 created and stored with my other lesson artifacts.

Troubleshooting

Flipper records “something” but replay does nothing

  • Get closer (2–6 inches) and re-record.
  • Try a darker room (sunlight can interfere with IR receivers).
  • Make sure you’re aiming at the device’s IR receiver (not just “at the TV”).

Recording fails / “timeout”

  • Hold steady and press the remote button once (no long holds unless prompted).
  • Replace remote batteries if it’s weak.
  • Try a different button to confirm the remote is transmitting.

AC remote is weird

  • AC remotes often send long “state” packets. Start with TV/LED remote if you want an easy first win.
  • If you stick with AC: record twice and compare results—consistency matters.
Reality check: If IR doesn’t work, it’s almost never “Flipper is broken.” It’s distance, angle, or the wrong receiver spot.

Finished Means

  • D3.1 + D3.2 + D3.3 exist.
  • You can repeat record → save → replay without rereading this page.
